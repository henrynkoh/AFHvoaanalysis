Let's break it down.

### What Are Bottleneck Scores?

Bottleneck scores range from **0-100**, where higher scores indicate greater bottlenecks (problems, friction, challenges) in that dimension.

**The 5 Dimensions:**
1. **Regulatory** (0-100): Licensing, compliance, bed fees, inspections
2. **Staffing** (0-100): Recruitment, retention, certification, training
3. **Financial** (0-100): Rates, costs, margins, budget impacts
4. **Community** (0-100): Trust, satisfaction, communication, relationships
5. **Tech** (0-100): Adoption, integration, training, automation

### Understanding Score Ranges

**üî¥ 80-100 (Critical)**
- Immediate action required
- Causing significant problems
- High priority for resolution
- May be impacting operations daily

**Example**: Regulatory score of 95/100 means providers are experiencing extreme regulatory pressure. Action needed immediately.

**üü† 60-79 (High)**
- Significant concern
- Should be addressed soon
- Monitor closely for escalation
- Plan intervention within weeks

**Example**: Staffing score of 85/100 indicates major recruitment and retention challenges. Develop solutions soon.

**üü° 40-59 (Medium)**
- Moderate concern
- Track for trends
- Plan improvements
- Address in normal planning cycle

**Example**: Community score of 60/100 shows relationship-building opportunities. Plan improvements.

**üü¢ 0-39 (Low)**
- Functioning well
- Maintain current state
- Opportunity for optimization
- Low priority

**Example**: Tech score of 30/100 indicates low technology adoption. Opportunity for competitive advantage.

### How to Use Bottleneck Scores

**Step 1: Identify Critical Areas**
- Look for scores above 80 (critical)
- These need immediate attention
- Allocate resources accordingly

**Step 2: Review Corresponding Topics**
- Check the topics table for details

- Review frequency and percentage

**Step 3: Implement Solutions**
- Use the strategic solutions map
- Contact recommended parties
- Research AI technologies
- Follow implementation guidance

**Step 4: Track Progress**
- Run regular analyses
- Compare scores over time
- Measure improvement

## Email Campaign Best Practices

### Subject Line Tips
- Keep under 50 characters
- Use numbers and emojis sparingly
- Create urgency when appropriate
- Personalize with first name
- A/B test different options

### Content Tips
- Clear value proposition
- Scannable format (headings, bullets)
- Single clear CTA
- Mobile-responsive design
- Personalization where possible

### Timing Tips
- Welcome: Immediately after signup
- Educational: Weekly or bi-weekly
- Promotional: Monthly or special occasions
- Re-engagement: After 30 days inactive
- Case studies: Monthly

### Segmentation Tips
- Segment by user type (provider, investor, etc.)
- Segment by activity level
- Segment by plan type
- Segment by interests
- Segment by location
